Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1: What is nanotechnology?

Nanotechnology is the science and engineering of working with materials at an extremely small scale, usually between 1 and 100 nanometers. At this size, materials can behave differently, allowing scientists to create new technologies with improved strength, conductivity, efficiency, and special properties.

2: Why is nanotechnology important?

Nanotechnology is important because it helps solve real-world problems using advanced materials and tiny-scale engineering. It is already being used in:

  • Medicine (drug delivery, disease detection)
  • Electronics (smaller and faster chips)
  • Energy (better batteries, solar tech)
  • Environment (water filtration and purification)
